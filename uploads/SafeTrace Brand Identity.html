00Brand essence

A calm ocean at twilight — deep, steady, protective, but warm.

SafeTrace exists for the most vulnerable moments in a person's life. The brand must hold both clinical authority and human softness in the same frame. We design for someone whose hands are still shaking.

Purpose

Hold the weight, so they don't have to.

Documentation, evidence, timeline, and access to care — quietly carried in the background while the person catches their breath.

Posture

Trusted, never towering.

The visual system reads as institutional-grade without ever feeling like an institution. Authority is in the craft, not in volume.

Promise

Control stays with the person.

Nothing is shared, escalated, or surfaced without consent. The brand makes this visible at every touchpoint.

01The mark

A shelter, a trace, a presence — held in one stroke.

The SafeTrace mark is built from a single fluid curve. A cradle wraps the user from below; the right arm lifts past the bowl, suggesting a path forward; a filled pulse marks where the person is, now. It is deliberately not a shield, not a lock, not a target.

  1. 1

    The cradle

    A wide, soft U-form built from one continuous stroke with rounded terminations. Reads as shelter, embrace, or held space.

  2. 2

    The trace

    The right arm rises 32 units past the bowl, suggesting a path or timeline moving forward — never finished, always continuing.

  3. 3

    The pulse

    A filled circle sits inside the cradle, slightly left of center. It anchors the person — present, accounted for, alive.

  4. 4

    The gradient

    A subtle twilight wash from Tidewater into Midnight Indigo. Never flat, never harsh. Mono fallbacks exist for embroidery, fax, and 1-bit displays.

The 70 / 25 / 5 rule

Use deep anchors (Midnight, Twilight, Paper) for ~70% of any composition. Tidewater/Aurora/Sage/Lavender share 25%. Coral never exceeds 5% — and only signals live urgency.

✓ Calm dominance
✗ Rainbow dashboards

Coral is a promise, not a color

Coral marks a live alert, an unverified incident, or a moment that needs the user's attention. It does not decorate. Pairing it with another saturated hue dilutes the signal.

✓ Single chip, single moment
✗ Coral CTAs everywhere

Lavender holds emotion

Reserve Lavender Mist for emotional-support and therapist surfaces (SafeConnect). It signals a softer modality and should never be used for billing, settings, or transactional UI.

✓ Therapy, journal, reflection
✗ Buttons, errors, charts
03Typography

A confident voice, set in a soft hand.

Three typefaces, no exceptions. A rounded display face that feels warm at scale, a humanist sans for everything the user reads, and a precise mono reserved for timestamps, evidence IDs, and any moment where accuracy matters.

Display · Bricolage Grotesque Variable · 12–96 opsz · 300–700
Held, not hurried.
Why this face

Bricolage is geometric without being clinical — its rounded apertures and humanist proportions read as confident in headlines and tender at large sizes. The variable opsz axis lets the same family hold both billboard authority and editorial intimacy.

Use for

Headlines, marketing hero, large numerical displays, section openers, the wordmark. Tracking: −2.5% at display sizes, −1.5% at H3 and below. Weight: 500 default; 400 italic for accent words.

Text · Geist Sans · 300–700
Read me
slowly.
Why this face

Geist's even rhythm and open counters keep dense product copy legible at 14–16 px. It carries warmth that institutional sans-serifs (Inter, Helvetica) lack.

Use for

Body copy, navigation, form labels, lists, transcripts, in-product UI. Default 16/24, weight 400. Buttons 14/20 weight 500.

05Brand voice

Four ways we choose our words.

SafeTrace speaks like a person who has done this before — and who has time. Never hurried. Never patronizing. Never asking for trust it hasn't earned.

01 — Pillar

Empathetic, never patronizing.

We meet people where they are. We don't soften reality, but we don't volunteer pain either. Plain language, no euphemisms, no "we understand how you feel."

Say

"Take your time. We'll wait."

Don't say

"It's going to be okay, sweetie."

02 — Pillar

Clear, never cold.

Short sentences. Concrete nouns. We name what is happening. But we also leave room — a single line break can carry care.

Say

"Your record is saved. Only you can share it."

Don't say

"Submission processed successfully (ID #4821)."

03 — Pillar

Protective, never controlling.

We default to private, encrypted, and reversible. We name the risk before we ask. The user can always undo, redact, or walk away.

Say

"This will share your timeline with your attorney. You can revoke access any time."

Don't say

"For your safety, we've shared this with our partners."

04 — Pillar

Empowering, always.

Control stays with the user — in copy and in code. We surface options, not directives. We name what the user is in charge of, every step.

Say

"You decide who sees this, and when."

Don't say

"Let us handle the next steps."

06Visual style

Generous, rounded, quietly luminous.

Six principles guide every composition — from the smallest in-product card to the largest billboard. If a layout cannot satisfy these, it goes back.

01 — Space

Breathing room is the brand.

Minimum 48 px gutters at marketing scale, 24 px in-product. Whitespace is not "empty" — it is the user catching their breath. Density signals an institution; air signals a person.

02 — Form

Soft geometry over hard edges.

Border radius is non-negotiable: 14 px UI, 22 px cards, 28 px hero containers. Sharp 90° corners read as legal paperwork; we are not legal paperwork.

03 — Light

Subtle gradients, never flat blocks.

Surfaces glow softly from one anchor color toward an adjacent one — never spanning the full palette. Think dusk on water: 8–14% lightness shift, single direction.

04 — Imagery

Photography reads as memory, not stock.

People half-turned. Hands at rest. Windows from inside. We never show faces in distress. Color grade pulls greens toward sage and shadows toward indigo. No clichéd "woman with phone" stock; commission real moments or omit.

05 — Motion

Slow, exhaled, never alarming.

Default ease: cubic-bezier(.2, .9, .25, 1). Durations 320–480 ms — long enough to feel deliberate. Pulses on safety states use a 2 s breath cycle. Nothing flashes. Nothing jumps. Nothing demands.

06 — Hierarchy of attention

Calm by default. Coral for one moment, then gone.

The screen sleeps in deep neutrals. Tidewater speaks for the product. Lavender or Sage colors a single chip when a soft state is named. Coral appears only when something is actually happening, and only for as long as it is — never as decoration, never as a wash.
